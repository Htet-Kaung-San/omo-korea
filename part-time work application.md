# Part-Time Work Permit for International Students (시간제 취업허가) — Accordion Content Draft

Source: 법무부 (Ministry of Justice) 시간제 취업 안내 (국문), Feb 2026.

**Read this first:** This is immigration law, not a campus procedure — getting
a detail wrong can mean a warning, a work ban, or in repeat cases loss of
student status (유학자격 취소). Treat this guide as an orientation, not a
substitute for checking with PNU's 국제처 (International Affairs Office) or
calling Immigration directly before applying.

---

## 1. Do You Need This? (기본원칙)

**Key facts:**
- Student visa holders (D-2) **cannot work or earn income in Korea** without permission — this includes part-time/casual jobs (아르바이트).
- With prior approval from **both** your university and the immigration office, limited part-time work is allowed.
- Specialized-field jobs (E-1~E-7 categories, e.g. language instruction) are generally **not** coverable under this permit — those need a separate 체류자격외 활동허가 (permission for activities outside your visa status).

---

## 2. Am I Eligible? (대상자 요건)

**Requirements — all must be met:**
1. **GPA:** C (2.0) or above in your most recent semester. *(Exempt for your first semester if no transcript exists yet — but your allowed hours are capped at half in that case.)*
2. **Korean ability** (or English track, see below):

   | Year | Required Level | Alternative |
   |---|---|---|
   | Bachelor's, years 1–2 | TOPIK 3+ / KIIP 3-step+ / Sejong Institute 중급1+ | — |
   | Bachelor's, years 3–4 & Grad students | TOPIK 4+ / KIIP 4-step+ / Sejong Institute 중급2+ | — |
   | Any year (English track) | — | TOEFL 530 (iBT 71) / IELTS 5.5 / CEFR B2 / (New) TEPS 327+ |

   If you don't meet the language requirement but meet everything else, you can still get a permit — just capped at **half the normal hours**.
3. **Stay duration:** Exchange/visiting students (D-2-8) must wait 6 months after their status change or entry date.
4. **Clean record:** No part-time work violation in the last 3 months.

---

## 3. How Many Hours Can I Work? (허용 시간)

**Base allowed hours, by course/year:**

| Course & Year | Weekday, requirement not met | Weekend/Break, not met | Weekday, requirement met | Weekend/Break, met | + Bonus tier weekday |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bachelor's, years 1–2 | 10 hrs | 10 hrs | 25 hrs | **Unlimited** | 30 hrs |
| Bachelor's, years 3–4 | 10 hrs | 10 hrs | 25 hrs | **Unlimited** | 30 hrs |
| Grad (Master's/PhD, any year) | 15 hrs | 15 hrs | 30 hrs | **Unlimited** | 35 hrs |

**Quick-reference weekly caps** (the same numbers above, restated as the official summary):

| | Bachelor's | Grad (Master's/PhD) |
|---|---|---|
| If requirement met (weekday max) | 25 hrs | 30 hrs |
| If requirement **not** met (weekday max) | 10 hrs | 15 hrs |

**The "+5hr bonus tier"** (rightmost column above): if you already meet the language requirement, you get **5 extra weekday hours** if you additionally qualify as any ONE of:
- Enrolled at a government-"certified" university program (인증대학 재학생)
- Straight-A (A grade or above) in your most recent semester (성적우수자)
- TOPIK 5+ / KIIP 5-step+ / passed the comprehensive KIIP evaluation (한국어능력 우수자)

**Weekend/holiday/break rule:** if you meet the base language requirement, weekends, public holidays, and official school breaks have **no hour limit at all** — this is an explicit degree-program preferential rule (학위과정 우대사항), not a loophole.

**Note:** You still need an actual part-time work permit even if you qualify for every bonus tier — none of this is self-executing.

---

## 4. What Documents Do I Need? (제출 서류)

1. Application form, passport, Alien Registration Card (수수료 면제 — no fee)
2. Transcript (성적증명서)
3. Proof of Korean (or English) ability
4. 외국인 유학생 시간제 취업 확인서 (International Student Part-Time Work Confirmation — your university issues this)
5. 외국인 유학생 시간제취업 요건 준수 확인서 (Compliance Confirmation)
6. Business registration copy + employer ID copy
7. Standard labor contract (표준근로계약서) — must state hourly wage, duties, and hours
8. *(Manufacturing/construction employer only)* Additional compliance confirmation

**Note:** The employment contract must be directly with the business on the registration certificate — staffing agency / dispatch arrangements where employer and workplace are separated **are not allowed**.

---

## 5. Where & How to Apply

1. Get your university's sign-off first — at PNU, this goes through **국제처 (International Affairs Office)**.
2. Submit to your local immigration office, or apply online via **Hi Korea** ([hikorea.go.kr](http://www.hikorea.go.kr)) — 민원신청 → 전자민원.
3. Approval covers **up to 2 workplaces** at a time, valid within your remaining stay period, max 1 year per approval.
4. **Changing jobs?** You must get a new part-time work permit *before* starting at the new workplace — this isn't automatic or retroactive.

**Busan Immigration Office (부산출입국·외국인청 종합민원센터):**

- Subway: Busan Station (Line 1) Exit 2, or Jungang Station Exit 14
- Hours: Mon–Fri 9:00–18:00 (lunch 12:00–13:00)
- Phone: **1345** (multilingual immigration helpline)

## 6. What's Off-Limits Even With a Permit (제한대상)

- Manufacturing and construction jobs, **unless** you hold TOPIK 4+ (construction is essentially never allowed regardless — even one violation results in an exit order, no exceptions for repeat offenses)
- Private one-on-one tutoring (개인과외) — restricted regardless of your field
- Adult entertainment/nightlife-adjacent businesses (bars with hostessing, massage/bathhouses, motels, gaming rooms, dance halls, etc.)
- Gig-platform jobs: delivery riders, courier drivers, designated drivers, insurance sales, door-to-door sales
- Remote/telecommute-only positions
- Jobs found through staffing agencies or labor dispatch/brokerage

---

## 7. If You Break the Rules (위반 시 처리기준)

- **Working without any permit at all** = illegal employment. Even a first offense can lead to a formal penalty, and construction-sector violations lead to an **exit order regardless of offense count**.
- **Have a permit but violate its conditions** (wrong hours, wrong workplace, etc.):
  - 1st offense: formal warning
  - 2nd offense: barred from part-time work for the rest of your studies
  - 3rd offense: your **student status itself** can be revoked
